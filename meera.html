Maya was married to the village postmaster Lalan Singh and had been happily married for the past two years.

Meera had passed her intermediate with a first-class and wished to continue her studies further. She wanted to go to college, but her village had none, so she would have to leave for the city. Her father, the village head was a person with progressive ideologies, yet in her case, he was too concerned to let her go out, and survive alone. Although after many a persuasion by the sisters, Param Singh was finally convinced that Meera could handle things well.

 

Meera was a bright girl, she had lots of dreams and goals to achieve. She wanted to study, so much, that she could open a college at her village. She loved her home very much and felt the pain of leaving it, she knew there were others who wished to study further, but because the way to do that would be to leave their home, they would decide otherwise.

 

Meera was a very beautiful girl, she was not only intelligent but quick-witted as well, loved by all, her only flaw was she was adventurous and inquisitive, like a child. As a little girl, she would play in the forest and swim through the river only to see what lay ahead. Although she had grown up and tamed her wildness her inner spirit could never be tamed. she was a good person but objects like swords and guns would always excite her. Often as a child she would dress up as the Queen of Jhansi and fight the boys playing football or cricket in the fields, she would return with black eyes at times, but she would always win.

 

Meera had beautiful thick, long and dark hair, and her face was round and bright. Her big eyes were her most striking feature, and her smile would make everyone around happy.

 

Soon came the day when she would go and explore new lands, and fulfil her dreams. The entire village had come to see her off at the station, her father pulling back his tears, yet full of pride for his daughter and so was the entire village. Everyone blessed her and wished her happiness. Lalan Singh and Maya were dropping her to the college, thus accompanying her, Meera had never stepped out of the village before.
