Awakening: My Experience with Truth!

Shri Mataji is the Founder of Sahaja Yoga, a simple meditation technique that connects us to the Whole (The Universe). I had got my realization in Sahaj (effortlessly). The realization was quick and joy-giving, and so comforting (like a mother holding her baby with love and protection; I felt like the baby), I had been a 13-14-year-old during the time of my realization. I experienced a gush of cool wind flowing through my fontanelle bone, and that was the beginning of my journey.

 

In Sahaja yoga, we all are one big global family of brothers, sisters, uncles, and aunts; So traveling with this new family was a joy I had never before experienced. We had many small incidents throughout the trip with which our faith in our Primordial Power only increased. We reached the place and each Sahaja Yogi was in the present moment, thoughtless and happy.

 

Chhindwara is the birthplace of H.H Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi. I am blessed with Mother's love.

For I may have wandered for a 1000 years before coming to this realization that we are a part of God's plan and it is not the other way round.

 

Shri Mataji Nirmala Devi and the Divine Vibrations - Shri Param Chaitanya around Mother (This picture is completely raw and untempered, hence the Truth)

 

While in Chhindwara, a particular experience cleared me to my core; It was evening time, and I was wandering along the path towards my shamiana and had lost my way. A glorious downpour had cut down our power supply and except for the main hall where the cultural programme was held, no other tent had electricity. I started running in between the rain, drenched and looking for my tent in the beautiful dark evening. I couldn't find anyone anywhere. I always loved getting drenched in the rain, this was different, the feeling was magnificent, every drop felt like it was soaking away the heat that my mind and body had accumulated in years.

 

Even though I couldn't see the way to the hall I felt extremely secure, I removed my footwear and kept walking and musing at the moment whilst the muddy earth soft and calming beneath my feet cleared me. I was thoughtless and yet very aware;

 

I don't know how I found my way to the hall, my sense of direction had become so clear all of a sudden; On reaching and entering the hall the rain stopped and I was the only person drenched from head to toe, I found everyone there, a huge festival, 'Jalsa' was awaiting. I was directed to my tent, I changed to dry clothing, and returned. I felt as if every cell in my being was rejoicing, thoughtlessly aware, and in the moment.

 

This was also a time when Shri Mataji was in her Sakara Swaroop and I witnessed her presence. Now that Mother is in her Nirakaar Swaroop I witness her as the joy in and around me at all times. I am blessed to experience the joy-giving Ritambhara Pragya flowing through each living being. I am Blessed! I am Blessed! I am Blessed! ( Just recollecting and writing this is making me so happy!! :D)

 

"When you are in meditation you must allow yourself to be in thoughtless awareness. There, the Unconscious Itself, the Achetana itself will take charge of you. You will start moving with the force of Achetana.

The Unconscious is going to work it out. It is going to take you there, where It wants you to go. You keep to thoughtless awareness all the time. Try to keep to thoughtless awareness as much as you can. When you are in thoughtless awareness you must know that you are in the Kingdom of God and His people, His arrangements, His consciousness, is going to look after you."

 

-SHRI MATAJI NIRMALA DEVI

 

Shri Param Chaitanya rising from Shri Mataji's picture and from the Hawan Kund

As a kid, I was always inclined towards Divinity. I could never understand though why God never spoke to us when we prayed. As a 3-4-year-old, I'd think that the Gods depicted on television were real, everyone at our home watched these shows very keenly. It was only for me to learn later that there were actors playing characters of Gods.

 

As I grew up I started reading about several epics and stories of God in Hindu literature; I had read the 'Bhagwat Geeta' while still in school. I was from Loreto Convent, so reading and learning about the Holy Bible were easy.

 

Despite coming from a strong Hindu family (Sanatan, as it's called), we've been quite liberal in learning and respecting all great religions. I learned about a majority of the Sanatan Scriptures, the Holy Quran, and its teachings from my grandparents and my father who were quite well-read in these areas. But that was not all, I liked reading Greek, Roman, Mesopotamian, and Chinese religious literature too. 'Existence' of life was a topic that kept me thinking for years.

 

So when I received my Self-realization in Sahaja Yoga, everything changed for the better because I learnt about spirituality, which is a direct path towards God, and Sahaja Yoga is an amalgamation of all the great learnings on this earth, not to forget mentioning that it is most scientific. We experience the Shri Param Chaitanya, the energy that creates all, is all-encompassing, and is the Shri Ganesh Tatva to which we pray ( in temples or mosques or churches or elsewhere) on our central nervous system. This energy is the Truth, pure Love and innocence, and is all-pervading.

 

This is the pure 'Rooh' as described in the Quran, and is the Holy Ghost mentioned in the Holy Bible and this is the 'Param Chaitanya' or 'Ritambhara Pragya' mentioned in the 'Saundrya Lahiri' by Shri Adi Shankaracharya.

 

It's blissful to know that although we all (people) are several, we are still the same universal Spirit on the journey towards 'Nirvaan' and 'Moksha' - Enlightenment and Freedom. Experiencing that, and helping others experience it, is Peace.

 

-Yavanika Kaushal
