Of our people and our Nation

'Tis true, politics don't interest me, perhaps because I don't care for change. However, at times, when I see some association with me and mine, I might have an opinion; And to my relationship with politics, that's the change.

 

I remember being a 5 or 6 year old asking my mother why'd the electricity go off for days. It would feel like Diwali when we'd finally have it back. 

 

'15 days ka load shedding, kya hi tha', does that even make sense. It didn't, but kya krte it was Lalu Prasad Yadav ka Gunda Raj. That's how it was, when Bihar alone existed.

 

The politician has been imprisoned so many times now that it's hard to believe he's ever had a home other than the Jail.

 

I am more relieved now than ever that Jharkhand separated from the once anciently glorious Bihar. Jharkhand isn't perfect but at least it's not a frog in the boiling pot of criminality yet, unlike what's to be of Bihar.  

Bihar most certainly would to be running into a pit of regression, allowing Lalu's family, known for all that's bad in our country, not only to stand but also win the CM's position, becoming the 1st among equals. 

The winning which may occur, seems to have nothing to do with the state's development or progress, but everything to do with 'Sharab or Daru' (alcohol) which would not be prohibited under the rule. That's a golden opportunity, isn't it!? 

 

All hail democracy, long live your sweet tyranny!

(Glad you appreciate my sarcasm!)

 

'Bhai, IIT sab band krwa do, sabhi lag jao daru peene me, Kon bol sakta hai yahan se IAS, IFS niklte hain!'.

Additionally, stands Yadav's audacious declaration that his Chief Minister's oath will take place this Diwali. Well exit polls or not, who cares right?

 

Get ready Bihar, to move backwards 15-20 years while your uneducated solicitors bask in your crimson blood. Freeing night creatures to torment you like they did more than 2 decades back. Moving out of your home after sundown will certainly be a threat to your and yours's. Who knows what the daylight would be like.

Bihar, with Patliputra as its head, a city that remained a capital to India's greatest dynasties, the land once as glorious to nourish the genius of Arya Bhatt, Chanakya and countless more. The Land of Rishi Valmiki where he wrote the auspicious and revered 'Ramayana' , The land of Kalidasa, one of the greatest dramatists and libertarians of the ancient world, The Mauryan and Gupta dynasties, under whom our  Bharat, our India flourished. The land of the Buddha, even Mahavira, the spiritual Peers and Sufis, and freedom fighters (in the more recent times) with the richest knowledge banks the world had ever seen under the greatest University of all times, Nalanda, might find no respite.

Seeing it march towards it's fatal ruins is disheartening. Owing mostly to its drunk, uneducated and short sighted masses who'd sell off their countrymen for some alcohol. 

 

I don't claim that previous governments were knights in shining armours, but couldn't they be called the lesser evil. I don't know, maybe.

 

Well, the people deserve that, if open debauched burglaries and loose dacoity is what they choose over the progress of their country. The sad part is that the people who choose better would have to share the same fate.

 

- Yavanika Kaushal
